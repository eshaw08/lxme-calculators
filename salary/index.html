What's the Golden Rule of Budgeting?

50:30:20 budgeting rule is the golden rule that helps you keep track of your expenses and maintain a budget. It guides you to divide your take-home pay into three categories: needs, wants, and savings & investments.

50% for Needs:

Allocate half of your take-home pay to essential expenses like groceries, rent, utilities, transportation, child care, etc.

30% for Wants:

Use 30% of your income for non-essential expenses/luxuries like dining out, vacations, and entertainment.

20% for Savings and Investments:

Save and invest at least 20% of your income for your financial goals.

This is a starting point and a thumb rule, you can always customise this as per your financial situation. It ensures you cover necessities, enjoy life, and secure your financial future.

Remember, to allocate a higher % towards savings and investments as your income increases!
